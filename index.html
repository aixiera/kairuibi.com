AI agent thinking

I treat agents like scoped workflow systems with clear handoffs.

The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to route the right work, keep the output useful, and give the human better context for the next decision.

Design rule System first

I optimize for workflows that are visible, scoped, and worth trusting.

My best work happens when a system has a clear job, a clear boundary, and an output that helps a person move faster without losing control of the process.

Explain the route Respect confidence limits Keep human context visible
01

Signal routing still needs logic

A useful agent needs thresholds, ranking rules, and fallback paths. If the system cannot explain why a source or summary is included, it is not ready to become a trusted daily workflow.

02

Research should become context

Retrieval is only valuable when it compresses into the next decision. I focus on turning scattered sources into briefings, pre-call notes, and outputs a human can actually use.

03

Human-in-the-loop stays visible

Not every step should be automated. Review points, approval moments, and confidence boundaries make the system safer and easier to trust.

04

Privacy and process come early

Workflow design has to account for what data is touched, where outputs go, and what should remain internal before the automation ever looks impressive.
