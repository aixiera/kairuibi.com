AI agent thinking

I treat agents like workflow systems, not magic tricks.

The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to route the right work, keep the output usable, and leave the human with better context.

Design rule System first

I optimize for workflows that are legible, scoped, and worth trusting.

The strongest AI work on this page is not about pretending the system can do everything. It is about defining the task boundary, making the handoffs obvious, and producing outputs that help the next human decision happen faster.

01

Qualification logic matters

A useful agent needs thresholds, branch rules, and fallback paths. If the system cannot explain why a lead is routed or rejected, it is not ready to help a business process.

02

Research should become context

Retrieval is only valuable when it compresses into the next decision. I focus on turning scattered sources into briefings, pre-call notes, and outputs a human can actually use.

03

Human-in-the-loop stays visible

Not every step should be automated. Review points, approval moments, and confidence boundaries make the system safer and easier to trust.

04

Privacy and process come early

Workflow design has to account for what data is touched, where outputs go, and what should remain internal before the automation ever looks impressive.
