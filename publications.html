A comparative study on facial expression recognition using MobileNetV2, VGG-16, ResNet and Swin Transformer

Published in Proceedings of SPIE Volume 13545 with Kairui Bi, Jiapei Liao, Dongting Gao, Ruillin Yin, and Yize Wang.

This paper compares CNN-based facial expression recognition models against a Swin Transformer on FER2013, using pretrained models, augmentation, at least 20 training epochs, and evaluation across accuracy, F1 score, precision, recall, loss curves, and confusion matrices.

Abstract

The paper studies the tradeoff between classical CNNs and a transformer-based approach.

Instead of assuming a newer model is automatically better, the work is set up as a structured comparison between established CNN baselines and a Swin Transformer for facial emotion recognition.

Summary Paper overview

Facial expression recognition continues to matter across security, identification, and broader computer-vision applications. This paper compares CNN-based architectures that are strong local-feature extractors with a Swin Transformer approach that is often motivated by stronger global feature handling.

Using FER2013, pretrained models, augmentation, and multi-metric evaluation, the study frames the comparison around practical strengths and weaknesses rather than model hype.

Method Evaluation setup
  • Pretrained MobileNetV2, VGG-16, ResNet, and Swin Transformer models.
  • FER2013 benchmark with 48x48 facial images across 7 emotion classes.
  • At least 20 epochs of training with augmentation and visualization-based review.
  • Performance measured with accuracy, F1 score, precision, recall, loss, and confusion matrices.
